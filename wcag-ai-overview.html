The Takeaway

Only 10% of WCAG 2.2 criteria (9 out of 86) can be fully checked by automated tools. Another 29% can be partially flagged, but still need human verification.

That means 60% of all success criteria require human judgment: manual keyboard testing, screen reader walkthroughs, cognitive review, and testing with real users who have disabilities.

AI is an essential first line of defence for catching the obvious. But full WCAG compliance requires a layered strategy: automated scans in CI/CD, augmented by structured manual audits.
